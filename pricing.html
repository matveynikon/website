How to calculate the price per run?
Before calculating the price it would take to rank your video, you must first calculate the amount of daily runs you would need to rank for your keyword. First, you have to select the keyword that you want to rank for. If the keyword you want to rank for is quite competitive then you would want to set more daily runs (the more days the higher you'll rank) so that youtube will see that your video is accelerating fast, thus would increase you're ranking. It would take around 2-4 weeks to properly rank your video and once you have finally ranked for that keyword, you can stop running this service until the next time you would want to rank.
The cost of one run can vary depending on the length of your video. If your video is 1 minute long, 1 run will cost 1 cent. Or if your video is 10 minutes long, 1 run would cost 10 cents. the less the cost of one run, the more runs per day you need to run, and vice versa. To calculate the price of one run for your video, you have to perform this simple equation:
(the length of your video(in minutes) * the number of daily runs) * price per minute = the price per day of ranking
eg. let's say I want to rank a video, the video is 7 minutes long, and the keyword I want to rank for is medium-competitive (see tutorial). This means that I should set my daily runs higher for the first 3-5 days. Because the video is 7 minutes long, the price per one run will be 14 cents. after those 3-5 days have passed, you can lower the daily runs until your video is ranked.
